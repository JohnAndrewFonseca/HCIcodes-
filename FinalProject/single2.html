Keyboard Switches

The little part that attaches to the keyboard PCB and rests beneath the keycap is called a keyboard switch. Each key on a mechanical keyboard has a separate switch. Switches come in many colors, shapes, sizes, and textures. They mostly go unnoticed, yet they have a significant impact on the feel and sound of your keyboard.

In general, switches may be divided into three sorts based on their design: tactile, linear, and clicky. Tactile: has a tactile bump just before the key comes out. Linear: features a tactile bump-free, smooth keypress Clicky: has both an audible click sounds and a tactile bump.
