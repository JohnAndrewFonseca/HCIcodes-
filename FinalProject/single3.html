Keyboard Keycaps

Keycaps are the front face of any keyboard and have a significant influence on the board's overall feel, sound, and aesthetic. Before deciding which keycaps should you get, you should also consider the different types of keycap profiles.

Now, what is keycap profiles? The part where things get a bit complex is the keycap profile. The term "keycap profile" describes the general form of the keycap; they might be spherical, flat, or have a variety of other forms. In certain layouts, the height of each row of keys varies to provide a concave or sloping curve across the keycap set. You may achieve a completely different look and feel for your keyboard by altering the shape of your keycaps.

Keysets often include keycaps for purchase. Keys in a keyset have common characteristics such as profile and colour. There are kits that classify the keycaps inside a keyset according to the keys they cover. You may purchase a Base kit that covers the majority of typical board layouts, whether you use a TKL, full-size, 60% board, or something in between with standard key sizes.
